Here you get an insight into my way of working with the mobile app "Made in Cologne".

For this project I created the concept, the UX and the UI design. The purpose of the app is to combine the functions of website, online store and museum guide. The goal was an optimal design for Android and iOS users.

structure of procedure

user personas and user stories

With the userflow as a basis, low and mid fidelity wireframes were initially created, which still form a lot of flexibility and customization possibilities. Here the focus was on the UX.

low and mid fidelity wireframes

Finally, I put the ideas from the mood board and the wireframes together, creating high fidelity wireframes. This was tested by several real users. They were given certain tasks on the basis of which they were supposed to judge the app. I collected and evaluated the feedback. I sorted all feedback that criticized the app's function or appearance according to relevance and adapted the design to the user's needs. The new design was tested again and feedback was collected again until the users were satisfied with the product.

moodboard

For the visual impression, I created a moodboard. I gathered inspiration from the web and researched the look of other museum apps. Since the "Made in Cologne" museum is mainly for young local artists, I decided to go with a fresh but unobtrusive color palette. This is done with black and white photos so as not to distract from the essence of the app. The typography is based on Android and iOS guidelines.
